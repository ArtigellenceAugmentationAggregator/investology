Buying residential property in your SMSF?
The borrowing window is closing.

From 10 August 2026, super funds can no longer take out new loans to buy residential property. If you want to use borrowing inside your SMSF, the contract has to be exchanged before the deadline — settlement can come later. That's weeks, not months.

What's actually changing
The rules in plain English

Here's the short version, in general terms — always confirm the detail with your licensed SMSF adviser or accountant before you act.

⛔ Ends 10 Aug 2026

New residential borrowing

New Limited Recourse Borrowing Arrangements (LRBAs) to buy residential property inside an SMSF can't be established from 10 August 2026. To use borrowing, the contract must be exchanged before that date — settlement can happen afterwards.

⚠ Higher risk

Rushed off-the-plan

Off-the-plan or to-be-built homes are risky here — if the contract can't be exchanged before 10 August, the borrowing option is lost. Time-to-exchange matters more than ever.

✓ Still available

Commercial & cash

Borrowing to buy commercial / business real property is unaffected. So are outright residential purchases made with the fund's cash (no borrowing). Plenty of pathways remain open.

✓ Protected

Existing loans grandfathered

Residential LRBAs and properties already in your SMSF before the date are protected and generally continue as normal. This is about new borrowing only.

Good to know
Deadline questions, answered
Do I have to settle before 10 August?
No — generally the contract needs to be exchanged (signed) before 10 August 2026; settlement can occur after. Confirm your exact position with your SMSF adviser.
What if I already own SMSF property with a loan?
Existing residential LRBAs are generally grandfathered and continue as normal — this change is about new borrowing only.
Can I still buy residential property in my SMSF after the date?
Yes — with the fund's own cash (no new borrowing). Commercial / business real property borrowing also remains available. Your adviser can map the options.
Is it even possible to move this fast?
Sometimes — it depends on your fund readiness, finance, and finding the right property. That's exactly what a buyer's advocate is for. Usha will give you an honest yes or no on your timeline.
